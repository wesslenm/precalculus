Example 3.17. Properties from graphs.

Use the graphs of trigonometric functions to find the following information:
(a) \(\tan 0\)
🔗
(b) All values of \(x\) in the interval \([-2\pi, 2\pi]\) where \(\sin x = 0\text{.}\)
🔗
(c) All values of \(x\) in the interval \([-2\pi, 2\pi]\) where \(\tan x\) is undefined.
🔗
🔗
Solution.
Looking at the graphs given earlier in this section, we can easily find the information.
🔗
(a) \(\tan 0 = 0\text{,}\) as seen from the graph of \(y=\tan \theta\text{.}\)
🔗
(b) Looking at the graph of \(y=\sin \theta\text{,}\) we can see that it crosses the \(x\)-axis 5 times in the inteval \([-2\pi, 2\pi]\text{,}\) at the \(x\)-values \(x=-2\pi, x=-\pi, x=0, x=\pi\) and \(x=2\pi\text{.}\)
🔗
(c) Looking at the graph of \(y=\tan \theta\text{,}\) we can see that it is undefined 4 times in the inteval \([-2\pi, 2\pi]\text{,}\) at the \(x\)-values \(x=-\frac{3\pi}{2}, x=-\frac{\pi}{2}, x=\frac{\pi}{2}\) and \(x=\frac{3\pi}{2}\text{.}\)
