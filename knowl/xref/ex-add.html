Example 2.12. Exponents and addition.

Are \((x^2+y^3)^2\) and \(x^4+y^6\) equal or different?
Solution.
They are different. In general:
\begin{equation*} (x^2+y^3)^2\not= x^4+y^6 \end{equation*}
We can test this by chosing some values for \(x\) and \(y\text{,}\) then checking what the expressions are. For example, let \(x=2\) and \(y=1\text{.}\) Then \((x^2+y^3)^2=25\) and \(x^4+y^6 = 17\) so they are not equal.
