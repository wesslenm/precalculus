Example 3.4. Radians on unit circle.

Draw the following angles (given in radians) on the unit circle:
\begin{equation*} \pi, \frac{\pi}{4}, \frac{7\pi}{4}, \frac{-\pi}{6} \end{equation*}
🔗
Answer.
🔗
🔗
Solution.
  • The angle \(\pi\) corresponds to half of a full turn (180 degrees).
    🔗
    🔗
  • The angle \(\frac{\pi}{4}\) corresponds to a quarter of \(\pi\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  • The angle \(\frac{7\pi}{4}\) brings us to Quadrant IV because it is only \(\frac{\pi}{4}\) smaller than a full turn.
    🔗
    🔗
  • The angle \(\frac{-\pi}{6}\) is in the clockwise direction, one 6th of \(\pi\) (and \(\pi\) corresponds to half of a full turn).
    🔗
    🔗
