Example 1.15. Simplifying a complex rational expression.

Simplify the complex rational expression:
\begin{equation*} \frac{\frac{x}{y}+1}{1+\frac{2}{x}}. \end{equation*}
Answer.
\begin{equation*} \boxed{\frac{\frac{x}{y}+1}{1+\frac{2}{x}}=\frac{x^2 + xy}{xy+2y}} \end{equation*}
Solution.
Simplify the numerator to make it a single fraction:
\begin{align*} \frac{x}{y}+1 &= \frac{x}{y}+\frac{y}{y}\\ &= \frac{x+y}{y}\text{.} \end{align*}
Simplify the denominator to make it a single fraction:
\begin{align*} 1+\frac{2}{x} &= \frac{x}{x}+\frac{2}{x}\\ &= \frac{x+2}{x}\text{.} \end{align*}
Now we are ready to simplify the complex rational expression. Remember that to divide by a rationl expression, multiply by the reciprocal (see (1.2.4)).
\begin{align*} \frac{\frac{x}{y}+1}{1+\frac{2}{x}} &= \frac{\frac{x+y}{y}}{\frac{x+2}{x}}\\ &= \frac{x+y}{y} \cdot \frac{x}{x+2} \\ &= \frac{(x+y)x}{y(x+2)} \\ &= \frac{x^2 + xy}{xy+2y} \end{align*}
There is no further simplification that can be made. So the final answer is
\begin{equation*} \boxed{\frac{\frac{x}{y}+1}{1+\frac{2}{x}}=\frac{x^2 + xy}{xy+2y}} \end{equation*}
