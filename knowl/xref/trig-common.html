Common angles.

You should be able to evaluate all 6 trigonometric functions at the following common angles:
  • First quadrant: \(\frac{\pi}{6}, \frac{\pi}{4}, \frac{\pi}{3} \) (Subsection 4.1.3)
  • Corresponding angles in other quadrants, such as \(\frac{5\pi}{6}, \frac{3\pi}{4}, \frac{5\pi}{3}, \) etc. (Subsection 4.1.4)
  • Multiples of \(\frac{\pi}{2}\text{,}\) such as \(0, \frac{\pi}{2}, \pi, \frac{3\pi}{2}, 2\pi, 3\pi \) etc. (Subsection 4.1.5)
  • Similar negative angles, such as \(-\frac{\pi}{6}, -\frac{\pi}{2}, -\frac{3\pi}{4} \) etc. (Subsection 4.1.4 and Subsection 4.1.5)
