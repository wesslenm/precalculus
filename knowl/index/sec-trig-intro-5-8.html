The unit circle can be used to define trigonometric functions. The unit circle is a circle with centre at the origin, and radius 1, as shown in Figure 3.3. The point \(P(x,y)\) in Figure 3.3 is the terminal point. The terminal point is defined as the point where the terminal side of an angle (in standard position) intersects the unit circle. The \(x\) and \(y\) values of the point \(P(x,y)\) will be important in the definitions of the trigonometric functions.
