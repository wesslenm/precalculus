Radian angles.

Radians are a measure of the size of an angle. In a circle, if you take the radius of the circle and lay that length along the circle’s edge, the corresponding angle is 1 radian.

The circumference \(C\) of a circle of radius \(r\) is \(C=2\pi r\text{.}\) This means that the angle corresponding to a full circle (360 degrees) is \(2\pi\) radians.
