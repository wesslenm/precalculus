Generalizing what we saw in Example 3.21, the graph of \(y=A \cos x\) (for \(A \not= 0)\) oscillates between \(A\) and \(-A\) (because it has been stretched vertically). Similarly for the graph of \(y=A \sin x\text{.}\) The value \(A\) is called the amplitude of the sine or cosine curve.
