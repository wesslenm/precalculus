Compared to \(y=\cos x\text{,}\) the graph of \(y=\cos (x+c)\) is shifted \(c\) units left if \(c > 0\) and right if \(c\) is less than 0. Here, \(c\) is called the phase shift or horizontal translation or sometimes horizontal shift. However, the term ``horizontal translation’’ applies to any graphs translated horizontally, while the term ``phase shift’’ is used only for sinusoidal graphs.
