Definitions of trigonometric functions - right angled triangles.

Consider a right angled triangle, where one of the accute angles is \(\theta \text{.}\) This means \(0 \lt \theta \lt \frac{\pi}{2}\text{.}\) Then the trigonometric functions are defined as follows:
🔗
  • Sine: \(\sin \theta = \) Opposite / Hypothenuse
    🔗
    🔗
  • Cosine: \(\cos \theta = \) Adjacent / Hypothenuse
    🔗
    🔗
  • Tangent: \(\tan \theta = \) Opposite / Adjacent
