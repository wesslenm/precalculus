Transformations.

Consider the functions
\begin{equation*} f\left(x\right)=a\sin\left(kx-d\right)+c \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} f\left(x\right)=a\cos\left(kx-d\right)+c \end{equation*}
🔗
If \(a\not= 0\text{,}\) the graphs are streched or compressed (compared to the graphs of \(f\left(x\right)=\sin x\) and \(f\left(x\right)=\cos x\)) so that their amplitude is given by \(|a|\) (the absolute value of \(|a|\)). If \(a\) is negative then the curve has been reflected in the \(x\)-axis as well as stretched or compressed.
🔗
The graphs are shifted up \(c\) units if \(c\) is positive, and down \(c\) units if \(c\) is negative.
🔗
Compared to \(y=\sin x\text{,}\) the graph of \(y=a\sin\left(kx-d\right)+c\) is stretched if \(k\) is less than 1, and compressed if \(k\) is more than 1. In either case if \(k\not 0\text{,}\) the period is \(\frac{2\pi}{k}\text{.}\) Similarly for cosine.
🔗
The period of \(y=\tan x\) is \(\pi\) so the period of \(y=a\tan\left(kx-d\right)+c\) is \(\frac{\pi}{k}\text{.}\)
🔗
To see the effect of \(d\text{,}\) it is easiest to first factor \(k\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} y=a\sin\left(kx-d\right)+c = a\sin\left(k\left(x-\frac{d}{k}\right)\right)+c \end{equation*}
Compared to \(y=\sin x\text{,}\) the graph of \(y=a\sin\left(k\left(x-\frac{d}{k}\right)\right)+c\) is shifted \(\frac{d}{k}\) units left if \(\frac{d}{k} > 0\) and right if \(\frac{d}{k}\) is less than 0. Here, \(\frac{d}{k}\) is called the phase shift or horizontal translation or sometimes horizontal shift. However, the term ``horizontal translation’’ applies to any graphs translated horizontally, while the term ``phase shift’’ is used only for sinusoidal graphs.
